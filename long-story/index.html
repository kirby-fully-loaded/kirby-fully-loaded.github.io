It's a Long Story

Once upon a time, a man decided to go back to school. He started with a community college, where he rediscovered his knack for language arts. When he neared the end of his two years at the college, he was recommended for a writing tutor position in the school's academic success center. He found he enjoyed helping other writers develop their ideas.

After his tutoring term at the community college, the man decided to continue pursuing a bachelor's in English at a four-year university and try his hand at an editing career. He took an editing course to get a better idea of the requirements and options, and thought freelancing would suit him best.

The aspiring editor thought it would be prudent to have a website, so he decided to take a web design course at the university. Even though it wasn't strictly necessary for his planned career, he reasoned that knowing how web design worked would at least make it easier to work with a professional if he contracted one to design a site for him. And if the editing career fell through, he'd have another skill he might make a career out of.

The web design course was enjoyable; the course professor was knowledgeable and kept assignments and lessons both interesting, which helped the man learn quickly. He wasn't a perfect web design student, but he made an honest effort to learn the material and keep up with assignment deadlines, even if he missed one or two.

At the end of the course, the man had an assignment in which he had to design a single, long webpage that contained five or more frames. The man wasn't sure what how to go about the assignment, so he just wrote an abridged story about his college experience and path to the web design course, and hoped the professor wouldn't mind the obvious references too much.

I enjoyed your course, Professor De Vries. I regret that your contract wasn't renewed; I would have liked to take a course on JavaScript from you, even if I'm not specifically pursuing a web design career. I'll keep a hopeful eye out, and maybe the university will reverse their decision. Either way, it was a pleasure learning from you, and I wish you good luck in your future endeavors.
