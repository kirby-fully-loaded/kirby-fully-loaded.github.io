Pacific Rhododendron

Quick Facts

  • Common name: Pacific rhododendron
  • Alternative Names
    • Coast rhododendron
    • California rhododendron
    • California rosebay
    • Informal: rhodie, rhodies
  • Scientific name:
    Rhododendron macrphyllum
    • Previously:
      Rhododendron californicum
  • Size range: 5-25 feet (1.524 - 7.62 meters)
  • Geographic range
    • Between Pacific coast and Cascade Mountains
    • British Columbia to Northern California
    • Range from coastal areas up to elevations of 6,000 feet (1.8288 kilometers)
    • In Washington State: Common in Olympic Peninsula and Cascade Mountain range
  • Climate range: Thrives in damp conditions, but tolerates dry. Prefers well-draining, acidic, low-nitrogen soils. Found abundantly in pacific coastal habitats within its range.

About

The Pacific Rhododendron is found along the Pacific coast, from southern British Columbia to northern California, and from the Pacific Coast up to an elevation of 6,000 ft (1.8288 km) in the Cascades. Within Washington State, it is most commonly found in the forests of the Olympic Peninsula and the Cascade Mountains, where it comfortably coexists with its fellow native plants: the confiers making up the bulk of these forests.

The Pacific Rhododendron was chosen as Washington's state flower in 1892, by a panel of about 15,000 Washingtonian women (men were not permitted to vote) so that Washington could be represented in the National Garland of Flowers exhibit for the Chicago World's Fair, to be held the following year. It remained the "de-facto" state flower until the decision was approved by the Washington Legislasture in 1949.
