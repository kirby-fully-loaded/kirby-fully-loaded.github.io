Elements and Backgrounds

The main differences between an html element, such as , and a background image background-image: is that a background image is meant to serve essentially as white space for the webpage, while a foreground element like img has specific attention drawn to it, often because it is directly important to the content of the page; for example, it could be a representation of something the webpage is trying to disucss or inform the audience of.

CSS Properties

Certain CSS properties can be applied to background images, often changing the way they appear. One example is applying a border to an image, border, which would help it stand out as distinct from the busier webpages, particularly if the page uses an image or video as its background.

Another example is the backdrop filter property, backdrop-filter, which can be used to apply a variety of effects between a foreground element and a background image. Like the border property, backdrop filters are useful for differentiating a noise background from the foreground, so that the foreground elements are more easily read.

Dimension and Resolution

In a sentence, dimension is the human-observable width and height of an image, while resolution is the ability of an image to maintain quality at differing sizes.
